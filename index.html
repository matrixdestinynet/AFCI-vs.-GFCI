GFCI
Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter

Protects PEOPLE from SHOCK

Detects ground faults‚Äîwhen electricity strays outside intended path (through water or a person). Trips in as little as 1/40th of a second, preventing fatal electrocution.

AFCI
Arc Fault Circuit Interrupter

Protects PROPERTY from FIRE

Detects arc faults‚Äîdangerous sparking caused by damaged wires, loose connections, or pinched cords. Interrupts circuit before arcing ignites framing or insulation.

How to Identify Them

GFCI Receptacle

  • ‚úì TEST and RESET buttons on face
  • ‚úì Usually located in bathrooms, kitchens, garages
  • ‚úì May protect downstream outlets

AFCI Breaker

  • ‚úì Test button on breaker itself
  • ‚úì Labeled "AFCI" or "Combination AFCI"
  • ‚úì Reset like standard breaker

Dual-Function

  • ‚úì Breaker with TEST button
  • ‚úì Labeled "AFCI/GFCI"
  • ‚úì Receptacles with TEST/RESET + AFCI label

Can't tell? Test it. Push the TEST button. GFCI receptacle should click and cut power. AFCI breaker should trip. If neither happens, device may be mislabeled, damaged, or wired incorrectly‚Äîcall an electrician.

Common Confusion, Critical Difference

‚ö†Ô∏è

GFCI Does NOT Detect Arcs

A GFCI will not trip on a smoldering cord behind your sofa. It only senses current leaking to ground‚Äînot dangerous sparking.

‚ö†Ô∏è

AFCI Does NOT Prevent Shock

An AFCI will not protect you from dropping a hair dryer in a sink. It detects series/parallel arcs, not ground faults.

‚úÖ

Both Save Lives

GFCIs have reduced electrocutions by 70% since 1970. AFCIs prevent an estimated 50,000+ fires annually. They are complementary, not interchangeable.
